Indian Cuisines

Indian food is known for its diverse regional cuisines, reliance on fresh spices, and a unique blend of six tastes. It's also the "land of spices," producing over 70% of the world's spices. Despite the popularity of "curry," individual dishes use custom spice blends, and many common ingredients like potatoes and tomatoes were introduced by the Portuguese.

India's vast geography and cultural influences have resulted in a wide array of regional cuisines, each with its own unique flavors and specialties.India is the world's largest producer and exporter of spices, with a long history of using them to create complex and flavorful dishes.Instead of a pre-mixed "curry powder," Indian cooks create unique spice blends for each dish, using freshly ground spices for optimal flavor.Indian food theory emphasizes a balance of six tastes: sweet, salty, sour, bitter, pungent (spicy), and astringent.
